The Apply Process

  • If you are taking a course that requires you to use one of the Linux machines on the CS LAN ...
  • you need to complete the Apply Process
  • You will find instructions for doing this here
  • After you complete this process, two account will be created
    • A Unix account
    • A Windows account

Problems with Accounts

  • From time to time, you may experience a problem with either of your accounts
    • Your Unix account
    • Your Windows account
  • Problems you might encounter could include
    • You forgot your password
    • You can't log on with your account
    • There is something wrong with your Unix course directory

Fixing Problems with an Account

  • The CS Operators are the only people ...
  • who can fix a problem with either of your CS accounts
  • If you need a password to be reset ...
  • you must go to the Operators' office ...
  • and present your student ID
  • This is for your protection
  • The Operators' office is located in the Unix Lab
  • To get to the Unix Lab
    • Turn left after getting off the elevator on the 3rd floor of the Science building
    • Walk toward the Computer Science office, which sits behind a large glass window
    • When you are in front of the window you will be in a hallway
    • Turn left and walk down the hallway
    • The Unix Lab is a few doors down on your right and has many computers
  • The Operators' office sits behind a window ...
  • at the far end of the Unix Lab
  • Your password will be reset to the default value "abcd_1234"
  • You will be asked to change this the next time you log in
  • If your problem does not involve reseting the password ...
  • send an email to operator@cs.umb.edu
